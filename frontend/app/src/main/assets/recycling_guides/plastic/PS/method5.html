Make a Mini Whiteboard Eraser

PS (Polystyrene) Plastic
Small pieces of foam can be repurposed into handy whiteboard erasers. They are lightweight, easy to hold, and effective for quickly erasing dry-erase markers during classes, meetings, or personal use.

Materials Needed

  • Foam block or sheet (clean and intact)
  • Scissors or craft cutter

Steps:

  1. Select and prepare the foam. Choose a piece of foam that is dense enough to erase markings effectively but soft enough to handle comfortably.
  2. Cut into eraser shapes. Cut the foam into small, hand-friendly rectangles or squares, about 5–7 cm in size for easy gripping.
  3. Smooth the edges. Trim or sand any rough edges to make the eraser comfortable to hold. Avoid sharp corners that might scratch the whiteboard surface.
  4. Test the eraser. Wipe a section of your whiteboard with the foam piece to ensure it removes marker clearly. Adjust the size or shape if needed.
  5. Use and store. Keep your mini foam eraser near your whiteboard. It can be reused many times and is easy to replace if worn out.
