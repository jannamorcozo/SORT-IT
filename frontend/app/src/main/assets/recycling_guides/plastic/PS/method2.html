Make a Pin Cushion

PS (Polystyrene) Plastic
Foam blocks or sheets can be easily repurposed into pin cushions for sewing projects. The dense foam holds pins and needles securely, keeping them organized and easily accessible while you work.

Materials Needed

  • Foam block or sheet (clean and intact)
  • Fabric (optional, for a decorative cover)
  • Scissors
  • Needle and thread (optional, for securing fabric)

Steps:

  1. Select and cut the foam. Choose a piece of foam that is dense enough to hold pins. Cut it into a convenient size, such as a small block, cube, or cylinder, depending on your preference.
  2. Wrap with fabric (optional). If you want a decorative pin cushion, cut a piece of fabric slightly larger than the foam. Wrap the foam completely and fold edges neatly.
  3. Secure the fabric. Use a needle and thread to stitch the fabric closed, or tuck edges securely around the foam. You can also use glue for a simpler finish.
  4. Insert pins. Place pins, needles, or small sewing tools into the foam. The cushion will hold them firmly in place for easy access.
  5. Use in sewing projects. Keep your pin cushion on your sewing table, in your craft kit, or near your work area to safely store and organize pins.
