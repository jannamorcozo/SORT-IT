Make a DIY Jewelry Organizer from Plastic Cutleries

PS (Polystyrene) Plastic
Plastic cutlery, especially forks, can be creatively repurposed into a simple jewelry organizer. This DIY project is lightweight, easy to make, and perfect for hanging earrings, bracelets, and necklaces while keeping your jewelry organized.

Materials Needed

  • Clean plastic forks
  • Small block of foam, cork, or sturdy cardboard
  • Glue (optional for extra stability)

Steps:

  1. Clean the cutlery. Wash the plastic forks thoroughly with soap and water. Dry completely to ensure they stick or stay firmly in place.
  2. Prepare the base. Choose a foam block, corkboard, or piece of cardboard as the base. Make sure it is stable enough to hold the jewelry without tipping over.
  3. Insert the forks. Push the prongs of each fork into the foam or cardboard, spacing them evenly. Ensure they are secure and stand upright.
  4. Optional reinforcement. For extra stability, apply a small amount of glue at the base of each fork before inserting it into the foam.
  5. Drape bracelets and necklaces. Use the handle of the fork to drape bracelets, watches, or necklaces. Arrange items so they don’t overlap too much, making them easy to access
  6. Display and use. Place the jewelry organizer on a dresser, vanity, or shelf for daily use.
