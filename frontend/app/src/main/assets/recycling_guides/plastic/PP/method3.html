Create Plant Pots or Seed Starters

PP (Polypropylene) Plastic
Polypropylene (PP) containers such as cups or small tubs are sturdy and lightweight, making them ideal for reuse as plant pots or seed starters. This simple gardening project supports sustainability while giving plants a healthy start.

Materials Needed

  • Clean PP cups or tubs
  • Nail, screwdriver, or heated metal tool (for drainage holes)
  • Soil or potting mix
  • Seeds or small seedlings

Steps:

  1. Clean the containers. Wash the PP cups or tubs thoroughly with soap and water. Rinse well and allow them to dry completely.
  2. Create drainage holes. Use a nail or screwdriver to carefully make several small holes at the bottom of each container. These holes allow excess water to drain and prevent root rot.
  3. Prepare the soil. Fill the container with soil or potting mix, leaving about 1–2 cm of space at the top. Lightly press the soil to remove air pockets.
  4. Plant the seeds. Place seeds into the soil according to the recommended depth. Cover gently with soil.
  5. Water lightly. Water the soil until it is moist but not soaked. Make sure water can drain through the holes at the bottom.
  6. Place in a sunny area. Put the containers in a bright location such as a windowsill, balcony, or garden area where plants can receive enough sunlight.
  7. Monitor growth. Check regularly for moisture and growth. Once seedlings are strong enough, they can be transferred to larger pots or directly into the ground.
