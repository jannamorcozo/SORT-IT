Return for Professional Recycling

Other Plastics
Returning water containers to professional recycling systems is the safest and most environmentally responsible option, especially for large refillable containers (such as 5-gallon/20-liter water jugs). These containers are commonly made from polycarbonate or other durable plastics that require industrial recycling processes. Water refilling companies already have systems in place to collect, inspect, and send unusable containers to specialized recyclers—keeping them out of landfills and preventing unsafe reuse.

Materials Needed

  • Containers still recognized by water refilling stations
  • Containers from known water suppliers or brands
  • Cracked, cloudy, expired, or no-longer-safe refillable jugs

Steps:

  1. Check return eligibility. Look for brand labels and ask your local refilling station if the container must be returned.
  2. Do not DIY recycle or reuse. Avoid cutting or heating; damaged polycarbonate is unsafe for food or water use.
  3. Rinse lightly. Empty the container and rinse with clean water only.
  4. Return to the supplier. Inform staff if it’s damaged or expired and return caps if required.
  5. Professional sorting and recycling Suppliers inspect containers—usable ones are refurbished, damaged ones are sent to industrial recyclers for proper processing.
