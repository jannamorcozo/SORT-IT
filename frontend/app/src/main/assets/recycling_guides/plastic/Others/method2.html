How to Convert Containers into Water Storage (Non-Drinking Use)

Other Plastics
When a water container is no longer safe for drinking but still structurally sound, it can be repurposed for non-drinking water storage. This reduces plastic waste while providing a convenient supply of water for household cleaning, gardening, or emergency flushing. Proper cleaning and clear labeling are essential to prevent accidental drinking.

Materials Needed

  • Mild soap
  • Clean water
  • Marker
  • Funnel (optional)

Steps:

  1. Inspect the container. Check carefully for cracks, leaks, or weak spots. Do not use containers with damage that may cause leaks.
  2. Wash thoroughly. Clean the inside and outside using mild soap and clean water.
  3. Dry completely. Allow the container to air-dry upside down. Make sure no moisture is trapped inside.
  4. Label clearly. Use a permanent marker to write “NON-DRINKING WATER” in large, visible letters.
  5. Fill for non-potable use. Use a funnel if needed to avoid spills. Fill with water intended only for cleaning or utility purposes.
  6. Use safely. Store separately from drinking water containers.
  7. Use for cleaning floors, gardening, car washing, or emergency toilet flushing.
