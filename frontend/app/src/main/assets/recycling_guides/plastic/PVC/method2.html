Make a Cable or Cord Holder

PVC (Polyvinyl Chloride) Plastic
PVC pipe segments can be upcycled into simple cable or cord holders to organize charging cords, computer cables, or wires. This DIY project keeps your workspace neat, prevents tangled cords, and makes it easy to access cables when needed.

Materials Needed

  • Small PVC pipe segments (about 5–8 cm in length)
  • Screws or strong adhesive (depending on mounting surface)
  • Optional: drill or screwdriver for installation

Steps:

  1. Select PVC segments. Choose small PVC pipe pieces that are wide enough for your cords to fit through comfortably. Cut them to a length of approximately 5–8 cm.
  2. Prepare the mounting surface. Decide where you want to place your cable holder (desk edge, wall, or shelf). Clean and dry the surface for proper adhesion if using glue.
  3. Attach the PVC segments. Using screws: Drill small pilot holes if needed, then screw the PVC segments securely onto the surface. Using adhesive: Apply strong adhesive to the bottom or back of the PVC segments and press firmly onto the surface. Allow the adhesive to set as per instructions.
  4. Thread your cables. Insert charging cords, computer cables, or wires through the PVC segments. The pipes act as holders to keep cords separated and tangle-free.
  5. Organize multiple cords. Use several PVC holders in a row to manage multiple cords neatly. Space them evenly for a clean and organized look.
