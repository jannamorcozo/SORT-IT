Make Plant Supports or Trellises

PVC (Polyvinyl Chloride) Plastic
PVC pipes can be recycled into sturdy plant supports or trellises for climbing plants and vegetables. This DIY project is ideal for home gardens, patios, or balconies, providing structure for plants to grow vertically while keeping them healthy and organized.

Materials Needed

  • PVC pipes or tubing (clean and free of cracks)
  • Saw or pipe cutter
  • Zip ties, string, or rope
  • Optional: tape measure or marker for precision

Steps:

  1. Select your PVC pipes. Choose pipes that are clean, sturdy, and free from cracks or damage. Pipes of 1–2 inches in diameter work well for most garden applications.
  2. Measure and cut. Determine the height and width needed for your trellis or plant support. Use a saw or pipe cutter to cut the PVC pipes to the desired lengths.
  3. Assemble the structure. Arrange the cut pipes vertically, horizontally, or in a grid pattern depending on the type of plants you’re supporting.
  4. Secure the connections. Use zip ties, string, or rope to firmly attach pipes together. Ensure all joints are stable and can support the weight of climbing plants.
  5. Place in the garden. Insert the trellis or plant support into the soil near the base of your plants. Make sure it is secure and upright.
  6. Train your plants. Guide your climbing plants, vines, or vegetables onto the trellis as they grow. Adjust as needed to provide proper support.
