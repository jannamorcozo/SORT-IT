Make a DIY Funnel

HDPE (High-Density Polyethylene) Plastic
HDPE bottles can be easily transformed into practical funnels for pouring liquids, seeds, powders, or other small materials. This simple upcycling project reduces plastic waste and creates a handy tool for household, garden, or kitchen use.Turn old newspapers into homemade recycled paper using simple items you can find at home.

Materials Needed

  • HDPE bottle (clean and intact)
  • Scissors or craft knife

Steps:

  1. Clean the bottle. Wash the HDPE bottle thoroughly to remove any residue or odor. Let it dry completely.
  2. Decide on the cutting angle. Determine how wide you want the funnel opening. Typically, cutting the top portion at a diagonal works best to create a wide mouth that narrows to the bottle neck.
  3. Cut the bottle. Using scissors or a craft knife, carefully cut off the top portion of the bottle at the chosen angle. Smooth any sharp edges to prevent cuts.
  4. Test the funnel. Ensure the funnel fits into the container you’ll pour into. The neck of the bottle acts as the spout.
  5. Use your funnel. Use the DIY funnel for pouring liquids, transferring seeds, filling spice jars, or any other household tasks where a funnel is needed.
