Make Storage Containers

HDPE (High-Density Polyethylene) Plastic
HDPE bottles and jugs (like milk jugs or detergent containers) are durable and can be repurposed into practical storage containers. This simple recycling project helps organize small household items, craft supplies, or tools, while keeping plastic out of the landfill.

Materials Needed

  • HDPE bottles or jugs (clean and intact)
  • Scissors or utility knife
  • Glue or tape (optional, for reinforcement or stacking)
  • Marker or labels (optional, for organization)

Steps:

  1. Inspect the container. Check the HDPE bottle or jug for cracks, holes, or damage. Only use containers that are structurally sound to ensure your storage is sturdy.
  2. Clean thoroughly. Wash the bottle or jug inside and out with soap and water. Remove any labels if desired. Let it dry completely.
  3. Cut to size (if needed). If you want an open-top container, use scissors or a utility knife to carefully cut off the top portion. Smooth any sharp edges to prevent injury.
  4. Optional reinforcement. For extra stability, you can tape the edges or use glue to attach multiple containers together for modular storage.
  5. Use for storage. Fill the container with small household items, craft supplies, screws, nails, or other tools.
  6. Optional labeling. Use markers, stickers, or printed labels to mark what each container holds. This makes it easy to find items quickly.
  7. Organize multiple containers. Stack containers if the design allows, or line them up on shelves to create an organized workspace or storage area.
