Create a Hanging Herb Garden

PET (Polyethylene Terephthalate) Plastic
A hanging herb garden is a practical and eco-friendly way to recycle plastic bottles while growing fresh herbs at home. This vertical garden saves space and keeps herbs within easy reach for cooking. You can hang it on a balcony, kitchen window, or backyard wall.

Materials Needed

  • Plastic bottles (clean and dry)
  • Scissors or craft knife
  • String, rope, or twine
  • Soil
  • Herb seeds or small seedlings

Steps:

  1. Clean the bottles. Wash the plastic bottles thoroughly to remove any residue. Let them dry completely.
  2. Cut side openings. Using scissors or a craft knife, carefully cut a side opening in each bottle large enough to plant herbs. This opening will be the front of your planter.
  3. Create holes for hanging. Make two holes on opposite sides near the bottle's top. These holes will allow you to thread string or rope through for vertical hanging.
  4. Thread the string or rope. Cut a long piece of rope or string and thread it through the holes of each bottle. Tie knots below each bottle to hold them in place vertically. You can stack several bottles to create a vertical herb tower.
  5. Fill with soil. Add potting soil into each bottle, making sure the soil reaches the opening for planting.
  6. Plant herbs. Sow herb seeds or transplant small seedlings into the soil. Common herbs include basil, parsley, mint, or thyme.
  7. Hang the herb garden. Securely hang the vertical bottle arrangement from a hook, rod, or railing in a sunny spot. Make sure it is stable and won't tip over.
  8. Care for your plants. Water regularly, ensuring the soil stays moist but not waterlogged. Harvest herbs as needed for cooking.
