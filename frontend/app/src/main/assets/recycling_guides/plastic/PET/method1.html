Create a Self-Watering Plant Bottle

PET (Polyethylene Terephthalate) Plastic
A self-watering plant bottle is an easy and eco-friendly way to recycle plastic bottles while keeping your plants hydrated. By using two bottles, water is gradually drawn up into the soil, providing a consistent supply to your seeds or small plants. This method is perfect for beginners, small indoor gardens, or windowsills.

Materials Needed

  • Two plastic bottles of the same size (cleaned and dry)
  • Scissors or a craft knife
  • String, yarn, or a cloth strip
  • Soil
  • Seeds or small plants

Steps:

  1. Prepare the bottles. Wash both bottles thoroughly and remove any labels. Make sure they are completely dry.
  2. Cut one bottle in half. Using scissors or a craft knife, carefully cut one bottle around the middle. This will form the top part (planter) and the bottom part (water reservoir)./li>
  3. Create the water reservoir. Fill the bottom half of the bottle with water. This will act as the reservoir for your self-watering system.
  4. Invert the top half. Take the top half of the cut bottle and invert it so that the bottle neck points down into the bottom half. The bottom of this inverted bottle will hold soil and plants.
  5. Insert the wick. Thread a string, yarn, or cloth strip through the bottle cap hole (or create a small hole in the cap). The wick should hang down into the water in the reservoir. This will gradually draw water up into the soil by capillary action.
  6. Add soil and plant seeds. Fill the top half of the bottle with potting soil, making sure the wick is in contact with the soil. Plant seeds or small seedlings according to the instructions for your chosen plant.
  7. Position your self-watering bottle. Place the planter in a sunny spot on a windowsill, balcony, or countertop. Ensure it is stable and upright.
  8. Monitor and refill water. Check the water level in the reservoir regularly and refill as needed. The wick will continuously draw water into the soil, keeping your plants hydrated.
