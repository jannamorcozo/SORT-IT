Make a Plastic Bottle Lamp

PET (Polyethylene Terephthalate) Plastic
Plastic bottles can be upcycled into decorative lamps using LED string lights. This project is simple, safe, and gives you a colorful, eco-friendly light source for your room, desk, or outdoor area.

Materials Needed

  • Plastic bottle (clean and dry)
  • LED string lights (battery-operated or plug-in)
  • Scissors or craft knife
  • Optional: paint, markers, or stickers for decoration

Steps:

  1. Clean the bottle. Wash the plastic bottle thoroughly and remove labels. Let it dry completely.
  2. Decide on openings. Depending on the desired effect, you can leave the bottle whole for a soft, diffused glow, or cut small openings/holes to let light shine in patterns. Use scissors or a craft knife carefully.
  3. Insert LED string lights. Carefully put the LED string lights inside the bottle. If the lights have a battery pack, make sure it sits securely outside or at the base.
  4. Decorate the bottle (optional). Paint the bottle, add markers, stickers, or wrap with paper to create interesting patterns. Avoid covering the LED lights directly to prevent overheating.
  5. Place or hang the lamp. Put the lamp on a table, shelf, or hang it using string for decorative lighting. Turn on the LEDs to enjoy your upcycled lamp.
