How to Select White Ledger Paper for Recycling

Selected White Ledger Paper
Not all ledger paper is suitable for recycling or reuse. “Selected” white ledger means choosing sheets that are clean, undamaged, and free of contaminants to ensure they can be safely recycled or reused.

Materials Needed

  • White ledger sheets
  • Flat surface for inspection
  • Optional: gloves (for handling old or dusty sheets)

Steps:

  1. Gather all available ledger sheets. Collect sheets from old notebooks, accounting books, or office stacks.
  2. Inspect each sheet individually. Look carefully at the front and back for stains, ink marks, heavy folds, or tears.
  3. Check for dirt or contaminants. Remove sheets that have glue, tape residue, sticky notes, or other non-paper materials.
  4. Examine paper quality. Choose sheets that are smooth, intact, and strong; avoid brittle or heavily wrinkled pages.
  5. Check paper type. Make sure it’s plain white ledger paper, not glossy, laminated, colored, or coated. These types are not suitable for standard paper recycling.
  6. Sort selected sheets. Place the clean, undamaged sheets in a separate stack or container for recycling or reuse projects.
  7. Optional – handle safely. If the sheets are old or dusty, wear gloves to avoid irritation.
