How to Make an Origami Flower

Mixed Paper
This simple origami flower is perfect for beginners and can be made using recycled coated paper. It works well as a decoration, gift topper, or part of a paper bouquet. No glue is required, just careful folding.

Materials Needed

  • 1 square sheet of coated paper (6x6 inches is ideal)
  • Flat surface for folding

Steps:

  1. Prepare the paper. Place the square paper flat on the table with the colored or printed side facing down.
  2. Fold diagonally. Fold the paper in half diagonally to form a triangle. Crease firmly, then unfold. Repeat with the other diagonal to form an "X" crease.
  3. Create a smaller triangle. Using the diagonal creases, gently push the sides inward so the paper collapses into a smaller triangle (this is called a preliminary base).
  4. Fold the side corners upward. With the triangle pointing downward, take the right corner of the top layer and fold it up to the top point. Repeat on the left side. You should now have a diamond shape.
  5. Flip and repeat. Turn the paper over and repeat the same side folds on the back to maintain symmetry.
  6. Fold petals outward. Slightly fold the top corners outward on both sides to create the look of flower petals. Adjust the angles depending on how open you want the flower to look.
  7. Shape the flower. Gently pull the petals apart with your fingers to give the flower a fuller, more natural shape.
  8. Final touches. Press the bottom lightly to flatten the base so the flower can stand on its own. You may also curl the petal tips slightly using your fingers for added detail.
