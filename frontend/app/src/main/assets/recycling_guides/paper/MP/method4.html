How to Make Gift Wrap Out of Magazine Paper

Mixed Paper
Using magazine pages as gift wrap or gift bags is a creative and eco-friendly alternative to traditional wrapping paper. The colorful images and bold text from magazines give gifts a unique, artistic look while helping reduce paper waste.

Materials Needed

  • Magazine pages (large, clean, and colorful)
  • Scissors
  • Glue or tape
  • Ribbon, string, or twine (optional, for decoration)
  • Ruler (optional, for neat folds)

Steps:

  1. Choose suitable magazine pages. Select large magazine pages without tears or heavy creases. Pages with bright photos or patterns work best for wrapping.
  2. Trim the paper to size. Lay the gift in the center of the magazine page and trim the paper so there is enough excess on all sides to fully cover the item.
  3. Position the gift. Place the gift face down on the magazine page so the most attractive part of the page will be visible on the outside.
  4. Fold neatly around the gift. Fold the long sides over the gift first, smoothing the paper flat. Then fold in the short sides, creating clean, sharp edges.
  5. Secure the edges. Use tape or a thin line of glue to secure all folds and seams. Press firmly to make sure the paper stays in place.
  6. Decorate the wrapped gift. Tie ribbon, string, or twine around the wrapped gift for extra decoration. You can also add a handmade paper tag.
  7. Final check. Smooth out any wrinkles and make sure all edges are sealed before giving the gift.
