How to Make a Placemats or Coasters

Mixed Paper
Placemats and coasters made from magazine pages are an easy and stylish way to recycle paper while adding color to your table. The glossy images and patterns from magazines create eye-catching designs, and adding a cardboard backing makes them sturdy enough for daily use.

Materials Needed

  • Magazine pages (clean and colorful)
  • Cardboard backing (from old boxes or folders)
  • Glue (white glue or craft glue)
  • Clear glue or sealant (optional, for protection)
  • Scissors or craft knife
  • Ruler and pencil (optional)

Steps:

  1. Plan the size and shape. Decide whether you are making placemats or coasters. Placemats: rectangular or oval shapes Coasters: square or round shapes Lightly mark the size on the cardboard using a pencil if needed.
  2. Cut the cardboard backing. Cut the cardboard to your desired shape and size. Make sure the edges are neat, as they will define the final look of the placemat or coaster.
  3. Prepare the magazine pages. Select magazine pages with colors or patterns you like. Cut them into the same shape as the cardboard or into smaller pieces if you want a collage-style design.
  4. Glue the magazine pages in place Apply an even layer of glue onto the cardboard backing. Carefully place the magazine paper on top, starting from one edge and smoothing it down as you go.
  5. Smooth out wrinkles and bubbles. Use your fingers or a flat object (like a ruler) to gently press out air bubbles and wrinkles. This helps the paper stick evenly and look clean.
  6. Trim excess paper. Once the paper is glued down, trim any excess paper around the edges for a tidy finish.
  7. Apply a protective layer (optional). Brush a thin layer of clear glue or sealant over the surface. This adds durability and helps protect against moisture and stains.
  8. Let it dry completely. Place the placemat or coaster on a flat surface and allow it to dry fully. Avoid stacking or using it until it is completely dry.
