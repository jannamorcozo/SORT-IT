Make Magazine Paper Baskets

Mixed Paper
Magazine paper baskets are a creative and practical way to recycle old magazines into useful storage items. The colorful prints add a decorative touch, while weaving the paper strips creates a surprisingly sturdy basket. These baskets are great for holding small items like stationery, craft supplies, or gift items.

Materials Needed

  • Old magazine pages (clean and colorful)
  • Scissors
  • Glue or tape
  • Ruler and pencil (optional, for equal strip sizes)
  • Flat surface for weaving

Steps:

  1. Select and prepare magazine pages Choose full magazine pages with bright colors and minimal tears. Flatten the pages to remove folds or creases for easier cutting and weaving.
  2. Cut the pages into strips Using scissors (and a ruler if needed), cut the pages into long strips of equal width, about 2–3 cm wide. Keeping the strips uniform helps create an even and sturdy basket.
  3. Fold the strips for strength. Fold each strip lengthwise in half, then fold again if needed, pressing firmly along the folds. This thickens the strips and makes them strong enough for weaving.
  4. Create the basket base. Lay several folded strips vertically on a flat surface, placing them close together. These strips will form the base of the basket.
  5. Start weaving horizontally. Take another strip and weave it horizontally across the vertical strips, going over one strip and under the next. Continue this pattern across the base.
  6. Build and tighten the base. Add more horizontal strips, pushing each one tightly against the previous strip. The tighter the weave, the stronger and more stable the basket base will be.
  7. Secure the base edges. Once the base reaches your desired size, apply small amounts of glue or tape at the edges to prevent the strips from shifting.
  8. Form the basket sides. Carefully fold the ends of the vertical strips upward at a right angle along the edges of the base. These upright strips will become the sides of the basket.
  9. Weave the side walls. Weave additional folded strips around the raised vertical strips, following the same over-and-under pattern. Go around the basket layer by layer, gently tightening as you go.
  10. Secure and finish the sides. When the basket reaches the desired height, glue or tape the ends of the woven strips neatly on the inside of the basket to keep them hidden.
  11. Trim excess paper. Cut off any extra length from the vertical strips and tuck or glue the ends inside the basket for a clean finish.
  12. Let the basket dry. Allow the glue to dry completely before using the basket to ensure it holds its shape and strength.
