Make a Small Planter or Seed Starter

Used Beverage Cartons
Used beverage cartons can be repurposed into small planters or seed starters, giving them a second life while helping your plants grow. This is a simple, eco-friendly way to recycle cartons and start seedlings indoors or on a balcony.

Materials Needed

  • Used beverage cartons (cleaned and dry)
  • Scissors
  • Soil
  • Seeds or small plants
  • Optional: tape or glue
  • Optional: paint, stickers, or markers for decoration

Steps:

  1. Rinse the carton thoroughly. Wash the beverage carton inside and out to remove any leftover liquid or residue. Let it dry completely.
  2. Cut off the top. Using scissors, cut the top portion of the carton to create an open container. The height can vary depending on your plant size (about 10–15 cm is ideal for seedlings).
  3. Create drainage holes. Poke 2–3 small holes in the bottom of the carton to allow excess water to drain. This prevents waterlogging and root rot.
  4. Fill with soil. Add potting soil to the carton, leaving about 1–2 cm from the top. Press lightly to settle the soil.
  5. Plant seeds or seedlings. Place seeds or small plants in the soil according to the instructions on the seed packet. Water gently to moisten the soil.
  6. Position your planter. Place the planter on a windowsill, balcony, or any spot with adequate sunlight for your plants.
  7. Optional decoration. Customize the outside of your carton planter using paint, stickers, or markers to make it colorful and personal.
  8. Care for your seedlings. Water regularly and watch your plants grow. Once seedlings are large enough, you can transplant them to larger pots or your garden, carton and all—the carton will eventually break down in soil.
