Make a DIY Bird Feeder

Used Beverage Cartons
Recycling used beverage cartons into bird feeders is a fun and eco-friendly craft that helps local wildlife. With a few simple materials, you can create a safe, homemade feeder to attract birds to your garden, balcony, or yard.

Materials Needed

  • Beverage carton (cleaned and dry)
  • Scissors
  • String or twine
  • Birdseed
  • Optional: tape or glue for reinforcement

Steps:

  1. Clean and dry the carton. Rinse the inside of the beverage carton thoroughly to remove any leftover liquid. Let it dry completely to prevent mold or spoilage.
  2. Cut openings for birds. Using scissors, cut small openings on the sides of the carton so birds can access the seeds. You can make one or more holes about 2–3 cm wide. Ensure the edges are smooth to avoid harming the birds.
  3. Create a small roof (optional). Fold the top flap of the carton over to form a roof. This helps protect the seeds from rain and prevents them from getting soggy.
  4. Fill with birdseed. Pour birdseed into the carton through the top opening. Fill it enough for the birds to feed comfortably but leave some space at the top.
  5. Attach string for hanging. Tie a piece of string or twine securely around the top of the carton. Make sure it is balanced so the feeder hangs evenly.
  6. Hang the feeder. Hang your bird feeder from a tree branch, balcony railing, or hook in a spot visible to birds but safe from predators.
  7. Observe and refill. Check the feeder regularly and refill with seeds as needed. Clean the carton occasionally to prevent mold or old seeds from spoiling.
