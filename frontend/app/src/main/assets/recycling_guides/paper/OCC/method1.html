How to Make a Pet House from Old Corrugated Cardboard

Old Corrugated Cartons
Turn old cardboard boxes into a cozy, DIY pet house for your cat, small dog, or other small pets. Using recycled materials keeps your project eco-friendly while giving your pet a fun space to rest.

Materials Needed

  • Old corrugated cardboard boxes (sturdy and clean)
  • Scissors or box cutter
  • Ruler or measuring tape
  • Pencil or marker
  • Glue, tape, or hot glue gun
  • Optional: Fabric scraps, newspaper, or cushion for bedding

Steps:

  1. Select suitable boxes. Check each box for sturdiness and cleanliness. Avoid boxes with water damage, heavy tears, or mold. Choose one large box for the main structure and a smaller one (optional) for additional compartments or reinforcement.
  2. Decide the size of the pet house. Measure your pet to ensure the house is spacious enough. Leave enough height and width for your pet to enter, turn around, and lie down comfortably.
  3. Prepare the entrance. On the front of the large box, draw the shape of an entrance (round, square, or arched) and carefully cut it out with scissors or a box cutter. Smooth sharp edges to prevent injury.
  4. Reinforce the structure. If the cardboard is thin, reinforce the walls and floor by gluing extra pieces of cardboard inside the base and sides. This helps the house stay sturdy and durable.
  5. Add a roof. If the box has flaps, fold them down to create a slanted roof, or cut and attach a separate piece of cardboard on top with glue or tape. Ensure the roof is secure but removable if you want easy cleaning.
  6. Optional: Make compartments or windows. For fun or ventilation, cut small windows on the sides or add a second box inside as a cozy compartment for your pet.
  7. Add bedding. Line the floor with newspaper, fabric scraps, or a small cushion to make the house soft and comfortable for your pet.
  8. Decorate (optional). You can decorate the pet house with markers, paint, or fabric for a personal touch—but make sure all decorations are safe for pets and non-toxic.
  9. Check stability. Before letting your pet inside, gently test the house’s stability. Make sure there are no loose edges or sharp points.
  10. Introduce your pet. Place the pet house in a safe, quiet area and encourage your pet to explore. Add treats or toys to make it inviting.
