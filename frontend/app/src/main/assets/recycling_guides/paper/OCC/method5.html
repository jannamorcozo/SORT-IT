Make Seedling Pots from Paper Bags

Old Corrugated Cartons
Paper bags are biodegradable and can be turned into small seedling pots for planting. This method is perfect for starting seeds indoors before transferring them to a garden or larger pot. The paper decomposes naturally in soil, making this a fully eco-friendly planting option.

Materials Needed

  • Paper bags (clean and sturdy)
  • Scissors
  • Soil
  • Seeds (vegetables, herbs, or flowers)

Steps:

  1. Prepare the paper bag. Flatten the paper bag and smooth out wrinkles to make cutting easier.
  2. Cut into squares or rectangles. Decide the size of your seedling pots (about 10 × 10 cm works well). Cut the paper into squares or rectangles.
  3. Shape the pots. Fold each square into a small cup shape. Fold the bottom edges inward and press firmly to create a secure base that will hold soil without leaking.
  4. Fill with soil. Gently fill the paper cup with potting soil, leaving a small gap at the top for watering.
  5. Plant the seeds. Plant one or two seeds in each paper pot according to the seed packet instructions. Lightly water the soil.
  6. Care for seedlings. Keep the pots in a sunny spot and water as needed until the seedlings grow.
  7. Transplant seedlings. Once the seedlings are ready for planting outdoors or in larger pots, place the entire paper pot in the soil. The paper will naturally break down, and the roots will grow through it.
