How to Make Picture Frames from Old Cardboard

Old Corrugated Cartons
Recycle old cardboard boxes into custom picture frames. This project is eco-friendly, easy, and allows you to create frames in any size or design.

Materials Needed

  • Old corrugated cardboard (sturdy and clean)
  • Scissors or box cutter
  • Ruler
  • Pencil or marker
  • Glue or double-sided tape
  • Decorative materials (optional: paint, markers, fabric, washi tape)
  • Photo or print to frame

Steps:

  1. Select the cardboard. Check the box for sturdiness and cleanliness. Avoid boxes that are bent, water-damaged, torn, or moldy. Use a thick piece for the frame’s base to ensure it holds its shape.
  2. Measure your photo. Measure the width and height of your photo or artwork. Decide on the size of the frame border (e.g., 1–2 inches wide).
  3. Cut the frame base. Using scissors or a box cutter, cut a rectangle from the cardboard slightly larger than the photo, leaving space for the border.
  4. Cut out the frame opening. Draw a smaller rectangle in the center for the photo to show. Cut it out carefully, keeping the edges straight and neat. This becomes the window of your frame.
  5. Reinforce the frame (optional). For extra strength, cut a second frame of the same size and glue it to the back of the first frame. This prevents bending and gives the frame a sturdy feel.
  6. Decorate the frame. Use paint, markers, fabric, or tape to decorate the front and edges of the frame. Get creative with patterns or colors that match your décor.
  7. Attach the photo. Place your photo behind the frame opening. Use glue, double-sided tape, or small corner tabs to hold the photo in place.
  8. Create a stand or hanging option. For a tabletop frame, cut a small strip of cardboard, fold it into a triangle, and glue it to the back as a stand. For a wall frame, attach a loop of string or ribbon at the top.
  9. Check stability. Ensure the frame holds the photo securely and stands upright or hangs properly before use.
  10. Display. Place your recycled cardboard frame on a desk, shelf, or wall and enjoy your eco-friendly décor.
