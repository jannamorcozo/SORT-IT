How to Make a Lamp from Carton

Old Corrugated Cartons
Recycle old cardboard boxes into a custom, decorative lamp for your home. This project is perfect for creating a unique light fixture while giving cardboard a second life.

Materials Needed

  • Sturdy, clean corrugated cardboard (thick pieces are best)
  • Scissors or box cutter
  • Ruler
  • Pencil or marker
  • Glue, hot glue gun, or double-sided tape
  • LED tea light or low-heat LED bulb (important: do not use incandescent or high-heat bulbs)
  • Decorative paper, markers, or paint (optional)
  • Craft knife (optional, for precise cuts)
  • Cutting mat or protective surface

Steps:

  1. Prepare the cardboard. Ensure it is clean, dry, and undamaged. Flatten and remove staples or tape.
  2. Plan the lamp design. Sketch your design (cube, cylinder, lantern, etc.) and choose the size based on your LED light.
  3. Measure and mark. Mark panels for all sides using a ruler and pencil. Add optional cutout patterns for light effects.
  4. Cut the panels. Cut along marked lines and trim rough edges. Use a craft knife for detailed cutouts.
  5. Assemble the lamp body. Glue or tape the panels together. Reinforce corners if needed.
  6. Create an opening for the light. Cut a hole or slot for inserting and removing the LED light.
  7. Add a base (optional). Attach a cardboard base for extra stability.
  8. Decorate. Paint or cover the lamp as desired. Let it dry completely.
  9. Insert the LED light. Use only low-heat LED lights and keep them from touching the cardboard.
  10. Test and display. Check stability and lighting, then place the lamp where desired.
