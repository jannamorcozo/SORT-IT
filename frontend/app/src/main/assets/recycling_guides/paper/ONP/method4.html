Use Newspapers for Composting

Old Newspapers
Old newspapers are an excellent source of “brown” material for composting. They add carbon to your compost pile, balance out nitrogen-rich “green” materials like food scraps, and help create rich, nutrient-packed soil for gardening. Composting paper also reduces landfill waste, making it an eco-friendly recycling option.

Materials Needed

  • Old newspapers (clean, without glossy inserts or heavy inks if possible)
  • Compost bin, pile, or container
  • Green waste (fruit and vegetable scraps, garden clippings)
  • Optional: water, garden fork or shovel

Steps:

  1. Collect newspapers. Gather old newspapers that are clean and dry. Avoid glossy magazine pages or heavily colored inserts, as these may not break down well in compost.
  2. Shred the newspapers. Tear or cut the newspapers into small pieces. Smaller pieces decompose faster and mix more evenly with other compost materials.
  3. Add shredded paper to the compost bin. Layer the shredded newspaper in your compost bin or pile. Aim for about one-third of the total compost volume to be “brown” material like paper.
  4. Combine with green waste. Add nitrogen-rich materials such as fruit and vegetable scraps, garden clippings, or coffee grounds. Layer browns and greens evenly for optimal decomposition.
  5. Moisten if needed. Lightly water the pile if it’s dry. The compost should be damp like a wrung-out sponge but not soggy.
  6. Mix regularly. Turn or stir the compost every few days to speed up decomposition and prevent odor.
  7. Allow compost to break down. Over time, the paper and organic materials will break down into rich, dark compost ready to use in gardens or pots. This process typically takes a few weeks to several months depending on conditions.
