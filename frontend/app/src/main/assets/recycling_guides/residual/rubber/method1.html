Rubber Hair Ties

Rubber
Old rubber items such as bike inner tubes, elastic bands, or discarded rubber strips can be repurposed into durable and stretchy hair ties. This project is eco-friendly, easy to make, and provides hair accessories that last longer than standard store-bought options.

Materials Needed

  • Old rubber strips or inner tubes
  • Scissors or utility knife
  • Needle and thread (optional, for reinforcement)
  • Sandpaper (optional, to smooth edges)
  • Ruler (optional)

Steps:

  1. Prepare the rubber. Clean and dry rubber strips or inner tubes. Trim damaged or thick sections.
  2. Cut to size. Cut strips 1–2 cm wide and long enough to wrap around hair. Optional: Sand edges.
  3. Form the loop. Fold the strip into a loop and slightly overlap the ends.
  4. Secure the ends. Stitch, knot, or glue the ends together.
  5. Finish and test. Check stretch and comfort. Trim rough edges. The hair tie is ready to use.
