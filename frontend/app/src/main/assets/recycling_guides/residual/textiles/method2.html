DIY Hair Scrunchies from Used Clothes

Textiles
Old clothes, sheets, or fabric scraps can be upcycled into soft and reusable hair scrunchies. This project is beginner-friendly, reduces textile waste, and creates practical hair accessories from materials you already have.

Materials Needed

  • Used clothes or fabric scraps
  • Scissors
  • Elastic band (about 18–22 cm long)
  • Needle and thread or sewing machine
  • Safety pin (optional, for inserting elastic)

Steps:

  1. Prepare the fabric. Wash and dry the used fabric. Cut a rectangle about 8–10 cm wide and 40–45 cm long.
  2. Fold the fabric lengthwise with the patterned sides facing inward. Sew along the long edge to form a fabric tube.
  3. Insert the elastic. Attach a safety pin to one end of the elastic and guide it through the fabric tube. Tie the elastic ends together securely.
  4. Close the scrunchie. Tuck one fabric end into the other. Sew the opening closed by hand or machine.
  5. Finish and use. Adjust gathers evenly. Your fabric scrunchie is ready to wear or gift.
