Reuse as Trash Liners

Clean and Dry Flexible Plastics
Plastic bags and larger flexible plastics can be reused as trash liners instead of being thrown away immediately. This simple practice extends the life of plastic materials, reduces the need for new trash bags, and helps manage household waste more efficiently.

Materials Needed

  • Clean plastic bags or flexible plastic packaging
  • Small trash bins (bathroom, desk, or bedroom bins)

Steps:

  1. Select suitable plastic bags. Choose bags that are clean, dry, and free from holes. Grocery bags, packaging bags, or other flexible plastics work well.
  2. Line small trash bins. Open the plastic bag fully and place it inside a small trash bin. Fold the edges over the rim to keep it in place.
  3. Use in low-waste areas. Use these liners in bathrooms, bedrooms, or desks where waste is usually light. Avoid using them for wet or heavy food waste.
  4. Collect dry waste only. Place dry trash such as paper, wrappers, tissues, or packaging inside the bag. This helps prevent leaks and odors.
  5. Dispose or recycle properly. Once full, remove the bag carefully. Dispose of it according to local waste guidelines or reuse again if still clean.
