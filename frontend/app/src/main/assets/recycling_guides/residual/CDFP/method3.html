How to Make an Outdoor Covers and Protectors from Tarpaulin

Clean and Dry Flexible Plastics
Thick tarpaulins are highly durable and water-resistant, making them perfect for outdoor covers and protectors. Reusing old tarps helps extend their lifespan while protecting equipment and materials from rain, dust, and sun exposure.

Materials Needed

  • Thick used tarpaulin
  • Mild soap and water
  • Scissors or utility cutter
  • Duct tape or strong thread
  • Needle or sewing machine
  • Grommets and grommet tool (optional)
  • Rope or bungee cords

Steps:

  1. Clean the tarpaulin. Wash the tarp with soap and water to remove dirt and debris. Rinse thoroughly and allow it to dry completely.
  2. Measure and cut. Measure the item or area to be covered. Cut the tarpaulin slightly larger to allow proper coverage and securing.
  3. Reinforce the edges. Fold edges inward and stitch them, or reinforce with duct tape to prevent tearing. For long-term use, install grommets along the edges if available.
  4. Secure the cover. Use rope, bungee cords, or ties through reinforced edges or grommets. Ensure the cover is snug but not overly tight to avoid stress on the material.
  5. Use as a ground sheet (optional). Lay the tarpaulin flat on the ground. Fold edges slightly inward to prevent water pooling.
