Leather Keychains

Leather
Small leather scraps from old bags, belts, or shoes can be turned into durable and stylish keychains. This project is quick, creative, and perfect for reusing leftover leather while making practical accessories.

Materials Needed

  • Leather scraps
  • Scissors or rotary cutter
  • Ruler and pencil or chalk
  • Needle and strong thread or leather sewing tools
  • Leather glue (optional)
  • Key rings or split rings
  • Hole punch or awl
  • Rivets (optional, for added strength)

Steps:

  1. Prepare the leather. Clean leather scraps and remove any dirt or debris. Cut the leather into strips, tags, or shapes (e.g., rectangles, hearts, or triangles) according to your keychain design.
  2. Punch a hole for the key ring. Use a hole punch or awl to make a hole at the top of the leather piece where the key ring will attach. Ensure the hole is centered and slightly smaller than the ring for a snug fit.
  3. Assemble the keychain. Attach the key ring or split ring through the punched hole. Optionally, fold a leather strip and secure it with glue, stitching, or a rivet to create a loop design.
  4. Decorate or reinforce (optional). Stitch around the edges for extra strength. Add small decorative elements like beads, embossing, or paint. Reinforce stress points with rivets for durability.
  5. Finish and inspect. Trim any excess threads or rough edges. Check that the key ring is securely attached and leather is stable. Your keychain is ready for use or gifting.
