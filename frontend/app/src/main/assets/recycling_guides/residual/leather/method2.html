Leather Bracelets

Leather
Old leather scraps from bags, belts, or shoes can be transformed into stylish and durable bracelets. This project is eco-friendly, creative, and perfect for making personalized accessories.

Materials Needed

  • Leather scraps
  • Scissors or rotary cutter
  • Ruler and pencil or chalk
  • Needle and strong thread or leather sewing tools
  • Leather glue (optional)
  • Rivets or eyelets (optional)
  • Clasp, snap, or button (optional)

Steps:

  1. Prepare the leather. Clean the leather scraps to remove dirt and dust. Cut strips to the desired width and length for your bracelet. Optional: Round or bevel the edges for a polished look.
  2. Shape the bracelet. Measure your wrist and adjust the leather strip length accordingly. Optional: Twist, braid, or layer multiple strips for a decorative effect.
  3. Assemble the bracelet. Use stitching, glue, or rivets to secure layers or ends together. Attach a clasp, snap, or button if desired for closure.
  4. Finish and inspect. Trim excess leather or threads. Check that all attachments are secure. Your bracelet is ready to wear or gift.
