Jewelry Holders

Leather
Old leather scraps from bags, belts, or shoes can be repurposed into stylish and durable jewelry holders. Leather provides a soft yet sturdy surface to store rings, earrings, bracelets, or necklaces, while adding a handmade, elegant touch to your organization.

Materials Needed

  • Leather scraps or old leather items
  • Scissors or rotary cutter
  • Needle and strong thread or leather sewing tools
  • Leather glue (optional)
  • Small hooks, rivets, or eyelets (optional)
  • Foam, cork, or felt (optional for padding)

Steps:

  1. Prepare the leather. Clean leather scraps and remove any dirt or dust. Cut leather into the desired shape for your jewelry holder (tray, roll, or panel).
  2. Add padding (optional). Attach foam, cork, or felt to the leather surface using glue. This provides a soft base for rings, earrings, or pins.
  3. Assemble sections. For necklace rolls, fold leather and sew edges to create compartments. For earring holders, punch small holes or attach small hooks/eyelets. For bracelets or rings, create strips or slots in the leather to secure items.
  4. Reinforce and decorate. Sew along edges or attach rivets for added strength. Optionally, emboss, paint, or add decorative elements to personalize.
  5. Organize your jewelry. Place rings, earrings, bracelets, or necklaces into their designated sections. Ensure items are secure and easily accessible.
