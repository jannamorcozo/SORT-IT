How to Make a Wire Jewelry Holder

Copper
Copper wires are one of the most valuable and recyclable metals. Instead of throwing them away, you can take stripped copper wires to a scrap yard or metal recycling center. Recycling copper conserves natural resources, reduces energy use, and may even earn you a small amount of money. This is a safe, environmentally responsible way to dispose of copper materials.

Materials Needed

  • Stripped copper wires
  • Container or bag for collecting wires
  • Gloves (optional, for safety)

Steps:

  1. Collect wires: Gather clean, stripped copper wires and store them in a sturdy container.
  2. Sort (optional): Separate thick and thin wires; remove non-metal parts.
  3. Find a recycler: Locate a nearby scrap yard and check their requirements.
  4. Transport safely: Use a bag or container; wear gloves if needed.
  5. Recycle & earn: Have the copper weighed and get paid based on market price.
  6. Follow rules: Don’t cut live wires or use illegal sources; dispose of waste properly.
