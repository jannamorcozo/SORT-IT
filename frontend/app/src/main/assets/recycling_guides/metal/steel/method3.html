Garden Tool Storage using Old Pots and Pans

Steel
Old frying pans, saucepans, and metal lids can be repurposed into practical garden tool storage. Hanging cookware on a wall keeps small tools organized, visible, and easy to reach while giving new life to unused metal items.

Materials Needed

  • Old pans or metal lids
  • Hooks, brackets, or sturdy nails
  • Drill (if mounting on walls)
  • Screws (if required)
  • Gloves (optional)

Steps

  1. Choose a storage location. Select a wall in a shed, garage, or covered outdoor area. Make sure it can support the weight.
  2. Install hooks or brackets. Mark hook positions, drill pilot holes if needed, and secure the hooks or brackets firmly.
  3. Hang the cookware. Hang pans by their handles. For lids or items without handles, drill small holes and mount them with screws or hooks.
  4. Organize your tools. Place tools, gloves, or small supplies inside the pans and arrange them for easy access.
