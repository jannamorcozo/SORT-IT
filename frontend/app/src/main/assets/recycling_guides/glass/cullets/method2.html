Aggregate Substitute in Concrete

Cullet Glass
Cullet glass (crushed waste glass) can be used as a partial substitute for natural aggregates in concrete. This practice reduces landfill waste, conserves natural resources, and can improve certain aesthetic and functional properties when done correctly.

Materials Needed

  • Clean waste glass.
  • Glass crusher or hammer and sturdy container.
  • Sieves (optional, for size control)
  • Cement.
  • Sand and/or gravel (for partial replacement).
  • Water.
  • Protective gloves, goggles, and mask.

Steps:

  1. Prepare the glass cullet. Clean the glass thoroughly to remove labels and residues. Crush the glass into small particles similar in size to sand or fine gravel.
  2. Sort by particle size. Sieve the crushed glass if possible. Use fine glass for sand replacement and coarser pieces for gravel replacement.
  3. Determine replacement ratio. Replace 10–30% of fine aggregate (sand) with glass cullet for best results. Avoid full replacement to reduce cracking and alkali-silica reaction (ASR).
  4. Mix concrete. Combine cement, natural aggregates, and glass cullet evenly. Add water gradually while mixing until desired consistency is reached.
  5. Place and cure concrete. Pour the concrete into molds or formwork. Compact gently to remove air pockets. Cure properly by keeping the concrete moist for several days.
