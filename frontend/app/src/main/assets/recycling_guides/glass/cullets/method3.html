Water Filtration Layers

Cullet Glass
Water filtration layers demonstrate how different materials remove dirt and impurities from water. This is commonly used for school projects and basic emergency filtration demonstrations. Note: This does not make water safe to drink without proper disinfection.

Materials Needed

  • Clean cullet glass (uniform size)
  • Washed sand
  • Gravel or small stones
  • Container, barrel, or filter box
  • Mesh or cloth liner

Steps:

  1. Prepare the cullet. Wash the cullet glass thoroughly to remove dust and residue. Allow it to dry.
  2. Prepare the container. Line the bottom of the container with mesh or cloth to prevent materials from escaping.
  3. Add the layers. Place gravel at the bottom for drainage. Add a layer of cullet glass above it. Top with washed sand.
  4. Set the filtration flow. Pour water slowly through the top sand layer and allow it to pass through the cullet and gravel.
  5. Maintain the filter. Rinse or replace the sand and cullet periodically to keep the system effective.
